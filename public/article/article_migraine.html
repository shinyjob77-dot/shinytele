What Is a Migraine?

Learn about common migraine symptoms, triggers, prevention support, warning signs, and when telehealth may help.

A migraine is a type of headache condition that often causes moderate to severe throbbing or pulsating pain, commonly affecting one side of the head but sometimes both sides. Migraine episodes may last for several hours or even days and can interfere with normal daily activities.

Migraines may occur with other symptoms, including:

  • Sensitivity to light or sound
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Dizziness or fatigue
  • Blurred vision or visual disturbances
  • Difficulty concentrating during episodes

Some individuals may experience a migraine aura, which can include temporary visual changes, tingling sensations, or speech difficulties before headache symptoms begin.

Common Migraine Triggers

Migraine triggers vary from person to person. While triggers do not guarantee a migraine will occur, they may increase the likelihood in some individuals.

Stress & Emotional Tension

Stress is one of the most commonly reported migraine triggers.

Poor Sleep or Sleep Changes

Too little sleep, disrupted sleep schedules, or oversleeping may contribute to migraine symptoms.

Dehydration or Skipping Meals

Low fluid intake and missed meals may increase migraine risk for some people.

Foods & Drinks

Some individuals report symptoms after consuming:

  • Alcohol
  • Chocolate
  • Processed or cured meats
  • Certain cheeses
  • Excess caffeine or caffeine withdrawal

Environmental Factors

Bright lights, loud sounds, strong odors, weather changes, and excessive screen time may contribute to symptoms in some people.

Supporting Migraine Prevention

Some people find symptom improvement through healthy lifestyle habits, including:

  • Staying hydrated
  • Maintaining regular sleep schedules
  • Eating meals consistently
  • Stress management strategies
  • Identifying possible symptom patterns or triggers
  • Following medical advice and treatment recommendations when appropriate

Keeping a headache or symptom diary may help identify patterns and support more informed conversations with a healthcare provider.

When to Seek Medical Evaluation

Migraine symptoms can sometimes overlap with other serious medical conditions. Prompt medical attention may be needed if symptoms are severe, unusual, or new.

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

  • Sudden severe headache ("worst headache of your life")
  • Weakness, numbness, facial drooping, or trouble speaking
  • Confusion or fainting
  • Vision loss
  • Fever or neck stiffness with headache
  • Severe headache after head injury
  • New or worsening symptoms that feel different than usual

Can Telehealth Help?

Some migraine concerns may be appropriate for telehealth evaluation, including symptom review, health education, medication review, and follow-up care. However, some symptoms may require in-person examination, neurological assessment, laboratory testing, imaging, or urgent medical evaluation depending on severity and clinical presentation.
