Struggling With Weight Loss? Personalized Medical Weight Management From Home

Tried dieting, exercise programs, or weight loss plans without lasting results? Feeling frustrated, tired, or struggling with cravings, low energy, or weight-related health concerns? You are not alone. Sustainable weight loss is not just about willpower, it is about the right medical support and a plan that works for you.

At TeleDNPnow, our Weight Loss Management Visits provide personalized, evidence-based medical weight management designed to help you reach your health goals safely and realistically, all from the comfort of your home.

I am Shiny Job, DNP, FNP-C, a Family Nurse Practitioner with 14 years of clinical experience helping patients improve their health through chronic care management, lifestyle counseling, and individualized treatment planning. My goal is to understand your unique challenges, review medical history, identify barriers to weight loss, and create a personalized plan focused on long-term success, not quick fixes.

Weight Loss Management Visits May Include:

  • Personalized weight loss evaluation and goal setting
  • Medical screening related to weight concerns
  • Nutrition, portion control, and lifestyle counseling
  • Activity and healthy habit support
  • Weight-related chronic condition review such as blood pressure, diabetes, cholesterol, and sleep concerns
  • Medication evaluation and discussion when clinically appropriate, including GLP-1 treatment options for eligible patients
  • Ongoing support, accountability, and follow-up care

No judgment. No one-size-fits-all plans. Just supportive, personalized care focused on healthier, lasting results.

For safe and effective long-term telehealth weight management, some patients may need to purchase basic home monitoring equipment when appropriate, such as a digital weight scale, blood pressure machine, thermometer, pulse oximeter, measuring tape, or blood sugar monitor depending on medical conditions and treatment plans. These tools help support safer care and better progress monitoring from home.

Important Telehealth Disclaimer: Some patients may require in-person evaluation, physical examination, laboratory testing, EKG, diagnostic testing, specialist consultation, or urgent onsite care depending on medical history, symptoms, treatment response, or medication safety considerations. Certain weight-related conditions may require additional diagnostic measures before treatment recommendations can be made.

Weight loss medications, including GLP-1 treatment options, may not be appropriate for everyone and are prescribed only when clinically appropriate after medical evaluation.
