Feeling Overwhelmed, Stressed, or Emotionally Drained? Start With a Mental Health Check-In From Home

Life can feel overwhelming at times. Stress, anxiety, low mood, trouble sleeping, burnout, poor concentration, or feeling emotionally "off" can affect your daily life, work, relationships, and overall health. Sometimes the hardest step is simply reaching out, but you do not have to navigate it alone.

At TeleDNPnow, our Mental Health Screening Visits are designed to provide a supportive, confidential space to discuss emotional wellness concerns, identify symptoms, and help guide you toward appropriate care and next steps.

I am Shiny Job, DNP, FNP-C, a Family Nurse Practitioner with 14 years of clinical experience providing compassionate, evidence-based care for a wide range of health concerns. My goal is to listen without judgment, understand your concerns, provide screening and education, and help determine the safest and most appropriate path forward for your wellbeing.

Mental Health Screening Visits May Help With:

  • Anxiety, stress, and feeling overwhelmed
  • Low mood, sadness, or loss of motivation
  • Sleep concerns and fatigue related to stress
  • Difficulty concentrating or emotional burnout
  • Life stressors affecting overall health
  • Screening for depression and anxiety symptoms
  • Guidance for appropriate follow-up care and referrals

You are not alone. Taking care of your mental health is an important part of your overall wellness.

Please Note: TeleDNPnow provides mental health screening, education, and guidance only. Behavioral health medications, psychiatric medications, controlled substances, narcotics, stimulants, or long-term psychiatric medication management will not be prescribed through these visits.

Important Telehealth Disclaimer: Some mental health concerns may require in-person evaluation, behavioral health assessment, therapy, psychiatric care, crisis services, or urgent/emergency onsite evaluation depending on symptoms and safety concerns. If your condition requires a higher level of care, you may be referred to a behavioral health provider, therapist, psychiatrist, urgent care, or emergency services.

If you are experiencing thoughts of self-harm, suicidal thoughts, severe depression, psychosis, or a mental health crisis, please call 911, go to the nearest emergency room, or contact the 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline immediately.

With easy scheduling, convenient telehealth visits, and self-pay options available, getting started with mental health support has never been easier.
