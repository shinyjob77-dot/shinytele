  • Lifestyle and nutrition counseling

Step 1: BMI Category

BMI < 18.5

Underweight

Not an obesity treatment candidate. Evaluate nutrition, medication effects, and possible medical causes.

BMI 18.5-24.9

Normal Weight

Provide preventive counseling, lifestyle optimization, and health maintenance guidance.

BMI 25-26.9

Overweight

Begin lifestyle program and review waist circumference for additional cardiometabolic risk assessment.

BMI 27-29.9

Assess for weight-related condition

Screen for hypertension, prediabetes, diabetes, dyslipidemia, obstructive sleep apnea, PCOS, coronary artery disease, osteoarthritis, or NAFLD.

Decision Point: Any Weight-Related Condition?

Yes

If BMI is 27-29.9 with a weight-related condition, the patient may be eligible for medication evaluation after full medical review.

No

Continue lifestyle intervention and schedule follow-up in about 3 months, or sooner if symptoms or risk factors change.

BMI ≥ 30

Likely eligible for obesity treatment

Consider lifestyle treatment plus medication evaluation when clinically appropriate and safe.

BMI ≥ 35 + comorbidity

Evaluate bariatric surgery referral

Discuss referral options when obesity-related conditions are present and the patient is interested.

BMI ≥ 40

Strong bariatric surgery candidate

Review bariatric referral, medical weight-loss options, safety, labs, and long-term follow-up planning.

Medication decision review

TeleDNPnow may discuss GLP-1 medications, topiramate therapy, or bupropion plus naltrexone when clinically appropriate. Medication selection depends on medical history, contraindications, labs, side-effect risk, access, cost, and patient goals.

Lifestyle plan

Every obesity management plan should include practical nutrition, physical activity, sleep, stress, hydration, and behavior-change goals that can be followed long term.

Follow-up plan

Follow-up visits review weight trend, medication tolerance, blood pressure or home readings when available, nutrition progress, lab needs, and whether the plan should continue or change.

Safety note

This page is educational and does not diagnose, prescribe, or replace a medical visit. Obesity treatment must be personalized after review of health history, current medications, pregnancy status, allergies, vital signs, and clinical risks.
