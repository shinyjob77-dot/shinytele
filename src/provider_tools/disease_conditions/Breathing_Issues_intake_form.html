Breathing Difficulty / Shortness of Breath Intake Form

Provider-focused telehealth intake form for asthma, COPD, vocal cord dysfunction, heart failure, GERD, and anxiety/dysfunctional breathing differential screening.
Emergency Warning: If the patient has severe respiratory distress, oxygen saturation <92%, blue lips, chest pain, confusion, fainting, or cannot speak full sentences, advise immediate emergency care / 911.
This form supports clinical documentation and decision-making. It does not replace provider evaluation, physical exam, diagnostic testing, or emergency care when indicated.

Condition ICD-10 Key Features / Clues Labs / Tests Common Medications / Dosing Referral / Escalation
Asthma J45.909, J45.901 Episodic wheeze, SOB, cough, triggers, reversible airflow obstruction. Spirometry with bronchodilator response, PEF monitoring, FeNO, CBC eosinophils, IgE, allergy testing, CXR. Albuterol 2 puffs 90 mcg q4-6h PRN; Budesonide 180-360 mcg BID; Fluticasone 100-250 mcg BID; Budesonide-formoterol 80/4.5 mcg 2 puffs BID or PRN; Tiotropium 2.5 mcg 2 puffs daily; Montelukast 10 mg daily; Prednisone 40-60 mg daily x5-10 days. Allergy/Pulmonology if uncontrolled, recurrent exacerbations, need for biologic, or unclear diagnosis.
COPD J44.9 Chronic cough, smoking history, persistent dyspnea, decreased breath sounds. Spirometry, ABG if severe, CXR. Albuterol 2 puffs q4-6h PRN; Tiotropium 18 mcg daily; Salmeterol 50 mcg BID; Fluticasone-salmeterol 250/50 mcg BID; Prednisone 40 mg daily x5 days for exacerbation. Pulmonology if severe symptoms, frequent exacerbations, oxygen need, or diagnostic uncertainty.
Vocal Cord Dysfunction J38.3 Throat tightness, inspiratory stridor, normal lungs, often mistaken for asthma. Laryngoscopy, spirometry often normal. No routine pharmacologic therapy; Ipratropium nasal spray 2 sprays each nostril BID if trigger-related. ENT/Pulmonology; speech therapy and breathing retraining.
Heart Failure I50.9 Orthopnea, edema, crackles, JVD, fluid overload, SOB lying flat. BNP, CMP, CXR, echocardiogram. Furosemide 20-40 mg daily titrate; Lisinopril 5-20 mg daily; Metoprolol succinate 25-200 mg daily; Spironolactone 12.5-25 mg daily. Cardiology; urgent evaluation if hypoxia, chest pain, pulmonary edema, or worsening fluid overload.
GERD K21.9 Heartburn, regurgitation, cough after meals or lying down, often normal lung exam. Trial PPI +/- EGD if refractory. Omeprazole 20-40 mg daily; Famotidine 20 mg BID. GI if refractory, dysphagia, GI bleeding, weight loss, or persistent symptoms.
Anxiety / Dysfunctional Breathing F41.9 Hyperventilation, dizziness, chest tightness, panic symptoms, normal lung exam. Clinical diagnosis +/- TSH, CBC to rule out medical causes. Sertraline 25-100 mg daily; Escitalopram 5-20 mg daily; Hydroxyzine 25-50 mg PRN. Behavioral health/therapy if persistent, severe panic, safety concerns, or functional impairment.

Telehealth Suitability Reminder: Mild/stable asthma, GERD-related cough, vocal cord dysfunction screening, and anxiety-related breathing concerns may be suitable for telehealth. Acute severe SOB, hypoxia, chest pain, suspected heart failure exacerbation, or rapidly worsening respiratory symptoms require urgent in-person evaluation.
